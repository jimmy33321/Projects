  • Brad Wheeler named interim dean for IU School of Informatics and Computing

    October 21, 2015

    Wheeler, who will also continue in his current position of vice president for information technology and chief information officer, succeeds Robert B. Schnabel, who steps down as dean Oct. 31.

  • IU seeks to inspire future scientists with second annual Science Fest

    October 19, 2015

    Albert Einstein and Richard Feynman will return as family-friendly "zombies" during IU Science Fest, which features over 100 fun-filled activities to spark imaginations and ignite a desire to learn.

  • IU-led collaboration provides first trans-Pacific 100G circuit dedicated to research and education

    October 16, 2015

    Indiana University and the Pacific Northwest Gigapop have launched the world's first 100-gigabits-per-second circuit connecting Pacific Rim research and education networks with their counterparts in the United States.

  • IU, NSA Crane enter landmark agreement focused on research, applied skills for students

    October 12, 2015

    Under a milestone pact, Indiana University students, faculty and researchers will use Naval Support Activity Crane as a lab for applied research in science, information technology, engineering, math and sustainability.

  • Todd Thompson named director of Indiana Geological Survey

    October 12, 2015

    The current director, John Steinmetz, who is also state geologist of Indiana, will step down from his post Oct. 31. Thompson received his doctorate in geology from Indiana University in 1987 and has worked at the survey for more than 25 years.

  • IU School of Informatics and Computing breaks ground on new building, announces $8 million gift

    October 2, 2015

    On Oct. 2, Indiana University Bloomington celebrated a groundbreaking and announced the lead donor on a new building for the IU School of Informatics and Computing. The $39.8 million, 124,000-square-foot Luddy Hall to accommodate the rapid growth of students, faculty and programs at the school is expected to complete construction in December 2017.

  • Expert in nerve and muscle regeneration to deliver 16th James P. Holland Memorial Lecture

    October 2, 2015

    A top researcher in the regeneration of the nerves that control muscles will present the 16th James P. Holland Memorial Lecture on Monday, Oct. 5, at Indiana University Bloomington.

  • Star Trak: October 2015

    October 1, 2015

    The highlight of October will be a series of close conjunctions of three bright planets in the morning sky before sunrise. Venus, Jupiter and Mars will converge above the eastern horizon, with Venus the brightest followed by Jupiter and Mars in that order of brightness.
