Leadership & Administration

Leadership on the flagship campus

Our provost

The Office of the Provost oversees the academic programs, research, and policies of 16 schools on the Bloomington campus.

The provost is the chief academic officer for IU Bloomington. She provides leadership in faculty promotion, tenure, and recognition, as well as university outreach, research initiatives, and student recruitment and retention. Her main goal is to improve the quality of education at IU Bloomington and maintain its status as a world-class research institution.

All deans and leaders of campus administrative units report to the provost.

Get to know our provost

Our vice provosts

Our seven vice provosts oversee specific priorities of the university, such as recruiting a diverse student body and securing top research opportunities for faculty.

Educational Inclusion and Diversity

Attracts and supports students from minority groups, with a focus on undergraduate programs such as the Hudson and Holland Scholars Program, the Groups Program, and the 21st Century Scholars Program.

Learn more about Educational Inclusion and Diversity

Enrollment Management

Recruits and enrolls the most diverse, high-ability students and prepares them for academic success through engagement, service, and cultural activities.

Learn more about Enrollment Management

Faculty and Academic Affairs

Supports faculty careers by enhancing productivity, recognizing excellence, cultivating leadership, and striving for a nurturing campus climate.

Learn more about Faculty and Academic Affairs

Graduate Education and Health Sciences

Oversees graduate education for all programs except for the professional schools, and oversees the health sciences initiatives for the Bloomington campus.

Learn more about Graduate Education and Health Sciences

Research

Offers consultation, proposal development, competitive internal funding programs, and infrastructure for a wide range of research, scholarly, and creative activities.

Learn more about Research Development

Strategic Initiatives

Oversees the tactical implementation of the strategic plan across the Bloomington campus.

Learn more about Strategic Initiatives

Undergraduate Education

Leads campuswide programs and initiatives for undergraduates, pre-college students, and adults who are continuing their education.

Learn more about Undergraduate Education

University-wide leadership

The Office of the President oversees Indiana University’s eight campuses, which include more than 5,000 faculty members, 11,000 staff members, and 100,000 students. The president is responsible for setting the university’s key missions—excellence in teaching, research, and engagement—and outlining his bold vision for the future of IU.

Get to know our president

Vice presidents help the university achieve these goals in target areas such as diversity, engagement, and academic affairs. They also help manage the university’s immense research and educational resources.
