IU Auditorium marks 75 years with 2015-16 season: Tony Bennett, musicals, magic and more

  • May 3, 2015

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

BLOOMINGTON, Ind. -- Indiana University Auditorium announces its 2015­-16 season, including touring Broadway shows, musical performances, holiday traditions and more, scheduled to run from September through April in conjunction with the celebration of the facility's 75th anniversary.

The wide range of events reflects the diverse performances throughout the IU Auditorium's history. Performances include the colorful and soulful Soweto Gospel Choir, celebrated operatic soprano Angela Gheorghiu, Straight No Chaser's 20th-anniversary show and many more. Family-friendly events include Broadway's "Annie," "Stomp" and a special 7 p.m. performance of "The Illusionists."

"IU Auditorium first opened its doors in 1941 and quickly built a legacy as our community's gathering place,” IU Auditorium director Doug Booher said. “Like those who came before us, we feel honored to serve as stewards of our iconic venue, and to present world-class performances for the university and community. For this season, we have selected shows that not only resonate with the refined and eclectic tastes of our patrons, but also pay homage to our long history of stellar performances. We invite everyone to come celebrate with us at this year's assortment of globally influenced, artistically significant and culturally iconic events."

"As a venue for students in the performing arts, the Indiana University Auditorium has played an important role in augmenting our facilities for training in the arts," IU President Michael A. McRobbie said. "It has helped to foster many meaningful academic partnerships between world-class artists and our students. And, of course, it has hosted a multitude of superb artistic performances and brilliant lectures that have enriched the lives of members of the IU and surrounding community.

"The major milestone of the auditorium's 75th anniversary gives us not only an opportunity to celebrate the auditorium’s rich history and proud traditions, but also to chart an ambitious course for its future."

To get the best possible seats, patrons may become subscribers by choosing five or more events between now and the end of August and can purchase these using a variety of payment options, including payroll deduction for IU employees and bursar billing for IU students. Patrons interested in one to four events may pre-order now, before single tickets go on sale to the general public in September. For more information, visit IUauditorium.com or call 812-855-1103.

The 2015-16 season features:

Tony Bennett
When: 8 p.m. Sunday, Sept. 20
Cost: General public: $58 to $78; IU Bloomington students: $30 to $68
Synopsis: With a six-decade recording career that has seen him become one of the most celebrated singers of our time, Tony Bennett is still a master of his craft. Audiences give this timeless, unparalleled performer ovation after ovation, whether Bennett is belting out "Fly Me to the Moon" unplugged or building audiences up for an unforgettable rendition of "For Once in My Life."

Angela Gheorghiu
When: 8 p.m. Wednesday, Sept. 23
Cost: General public: $38 to $62; IU Bloomington students: $20 to $41
Synopsis: Romanian-born Angela Gheorghiu is one of the most celebrated and gifted opera singers of our time. She burst onto the scene in 1992 and today is recognized as one of the great sopranos of all time. As an exclusive recording artist with EMI Classics, she has been awarded accolades including the Gramophone Award, Diapason d'Or Award, USA Critics' Award and Female Artist of the Year from the Classical Brit Awards. "It's hard to imagine anyone bettering Angela Gheorghiu ... her voice, feather-light and creamy yet with a core of steel ... simply unforgettable," according to a reviewer from The Observer.

Jazz at Lincoln Center Orchestra with Wynton Marsalis
When: 7:30 p.m. Sunday, Oct. 4
Cost: General public: $29 to $45; IU Bloomington students: $17 to $38
Synopsis: Living legend Wynton Marsalis is an internationally acclaimed jazz musician, composer, bandleader and educator and a leading advocate of American culture. He is the first artist to perform and compose across the full jazz spectrum -- from its New Orleans roots to bebop to modern jazz. The Jazz at Lincoln Center Orchestra features 15 of the finest soloists and ensemble players today. With their debut performance in Bloomington, the orchestra and Marsalis will use their immense talents to enrich the musical education of IU students in a series of residency activities and enrich the cultural landscape of our community with a performance at IU Auditorium.

"The Illusionists"
When: 7 and 10 p.m. Tuesday, Oct. 6
Cost: General public: $39 to $65; IU Bloomington students: $21 to $44; children $21 to $44 (only at the family-friendly 7 p.m. performance).
Synopsis: This mind-blowing spectacular showcases the talents of seven incredible illusionists. "The Illusionists -- Witness the Impossible" has shattered box office records and dazzled audiences of all ages with its powerful mix of outrageous and astonishing acts. Audiences will witness grand illusions, levitation, mind-reading, disappearance and a full-view water torture escape performed by Andrew Basso, who will hold his breath for more than four minutes. These world-class performers take their cue from the showmanship of the great illusionists of the past and pair it with a contemporary aesthetic.

"Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat"
Editor's Note: Due to a scheduling conflict, the Oct. 14 performance has been canceled.
When: 7:30 p.m. Tuesday, Oct. 13, and Wednesday, Oct. 14
Cost: General public: $39 to $65; IU Bloomington students and children: $21 to $44
Synopsis: Tim Rice and Andrew Lloyd Webber's "Joseph and the Amazing Technicolor Dreamcoat" is an enduring, family-friendly musical about the Bible story of Joseph, his 11 brothers and the coat of many colors. "Joseph" has become one of the most-produced musicals in history with more than 40,000 stock and amateur productions since the show was written and first performed in 1968. This energetic new production takes the show audiences know and love to a whole new level of audio and visual spectacle filled with songs including "Those Canaan Days," "Any Dream Will Do" and "Close Every Door."

Twyla Tharp
When: 7:30 p.m. Wednesday, Oct. 28
Cost: General public: $22 to $40; IU Bloomington students: $12 to $35
Synopsis: Indiana native Twyla Tharp and her company come to IU Auditorium in celebration of her 50th anniversary as a leading presence in dance. A flourish of trumpets heralds double premieres. John Zorn provides the show's vibrant introductory "Fanfare," followed by "Preludes and Fugues." The first dance is set to J.S. Bach's "Well-Tempered Clavier," and the raucous humor of New Orleans jazz runs rampant through "Yowzie," the second new dance. With immaculate technique and joy, her fierce dancers bring choreography to life. Tharp proves that time is her partner as she continues to deepen and expand the singular imagination that makes her one of the century's most treasured artists.

Dennis James Hosts Halloween
When: 8 p.m. Friday, Oct. 30
Cost: General public: $16 to $21; IU Bloomington students and children: $8 to $16
Synopsis: Cinema organist Dennis James returns to his alma mater to perform the family-friendly masterpiece that started his career in the 1970s. He makes Halloween come alive as he plays the auditorium's historic Roosevelt Pipe Organ to accompany the playfully spooky 1925 silent film "The Phantom of the Opera." James has built his reputation as an accompanist by using the organ, theremin and electronic sound effects to enhance the thrill of silent films. The tragic love story takes place in a Paris opera house, where a mysterious phantom terrorizes its occupants. Fueled by his lust for the young Christine Daaé, he forces the prima donna Carlotta out of the limelight and launches the stardom of his new affliction -- wreaking havoc in hopes of winning her affection.

Soweto Gospel Choir
When: 8 p.m. Friday, Nov. 6
Cost: General public: $22 to $40; IU Bloomington students: $12 to $35
Synopsis: The Soweto Gospel Choir has become known as the epitome of South African gospel ensembles, cultivating some of the most evocative sounds on earth. Soweto's performances are filled with earthy rhythms and rich harmonies, accompanied by a four-piece band and percussion section. This dynamic choir, with 52 members performing in six of South Africa's 11 official languages, is charting a meteoric rise to international fame, having performed alongside Bono and Queen; and for notable guests such as Nelson Mandela, Oprah Winfrey, Kofi Annan, Sidney Poitier and Archbishop Desmond Tutu.

Yo-Yo Ma: The BRIC Project
When: 8 p.m. Wednesday, Nov. 11
Cost: General public: $38 to $62; IU Bloomington students: $20 to $41
Synopsis: With The BRIC Project, beloved cellist Yo-Yo Ma brings together longtime collaborators and friends with whom he has explored international musical and cultural traditions, in a program including works from Brazil, Russia, India and China. In economics, these countries are deemed to be at a similar stage of newly advanced economic development, and at the intersection of ancient tradition and modern civilization. With The BRIC Project, Ma and his collaborators -- pianist Kathryn Stott, Brazilian guitarists Sérgio and Odair Assad, violinist and composer Johnny Gandelsman, sheng player Wu Tong and tabla virtuoso Sandeep Das -- bring a 21st-century perspective to these diverse cultural traditions.

"Chimes of Christmas"
When: 2 and 7:30 p.m. Saturday, Dec. 5
Cost: General public: $17 to $22; IU Bloomington students and children: $12 to $17
Synopsis: For generations, audiences have delighted in this uplifting, holiday spectacular featuring Jacobs School of Music ensembles and led by IUs Grammy-nominated Singing Hoosiers. Over the past few seasons, "Chimes of Christmas" has presented a fresh new look, an innovative repertoire and oodles of energy. A family-friendly event with special discounts for children, "Chimes" is the perfect way to start the season. With the addition of a matinee, it's easy for all to enjoy the holiday carols and surprise appearance of Santa. "('Chimes of Christmas') is known every year as the most cheerful and family-friendly event among all of the Bloomington public," Bloomington resident June Quivers said in the Indiana Daily Student. "I love attending it every year in time for the Christmas season, and I will continue going as long as they have it. It really is that spectacular."

Straight No Chaser
When: 8 p.m. Thursday, Dec.17
Cost: General public: $37 to $57; IU Bloomington students: $20 to $52
Synopsis: Tight vocals and loose humor have catapulted these IU-born musical masterminds from local legends to international superstars, leaving in their wake a slew of chart-topping albums including last year's "Under the Influence," which featured the breakaway holiday hit "Text Me Merry Christmas" that peaked at No. 28 on Billboard 200. Year after year, the group captivates audiences with clever harmonies and tongue-in-cheek charm. Their wildly entertaining a cappella concerts prompted The Trades to write, "Straight No Chaser endeared themselves to fans with 'we-don't-take-ourselves-as-seriously-as-we-do-our-music' antics, good humor and heartfelt moments of self-admission."

"Stomp"
When: 7:30 p.m. Tuesday, Feb. 9, and Wednesday, Feb. 10
Cost: General public: $49 to $62; IU Bloomington students and children: $21 to $42
Synopsis: Matchboxes, brooms, garbage cans, Zippo lighters and more fill the stage with energizing beats at "Stomp," the inventive stage show that blends dance, music and theatrical performance together in one electrifying rhythm. This family-friendly experience of percussion, movement and physical comedy has been inspiring world audiences for 20 years.

"42nd Street"
When: 8 p.m. Tuesday, March 1, and Wednesday, March 2
Cost: General public: $39 to $65; IU Bloomington students: $21 to $44
Synopsis: This quintessential backstage musical comedy classic is a song-and-dance fable of Broadway that includes some of the greatest songs ever written, such as "We're in the Money," "Lullaby of Broadway," "I Only Have Eyes for You, and, of course, "42nd Street." Based on a novel by Bradford Ropes and on Busby Berkeley's 1933 movie, it tells the story of a starry-eyed dancer named Peggy Sawyer who comes to New York to audition for a Broadway musical. When the star breaks her ankle, Peggy takes over and becomes a star. With a book by Michael Stewart and Mark Bramble, music by Harry Warren and lyrics by Al Dubin, this sparkling new production will be directed by co-author Mark Bramble and choreographed by Randy Skinner, the team who staged the 2001 Tony Award-winning Best Musical Revival.

The Chieftains
When: 7:30 p.m. Tuesday, March 8
Cost: General public: $40 to $55; IU Bloomington students and children: $19 to $40
Synopsis: Known as the best Irish band in the world today, The Chieftains have a range and repertoire that have captured a broad public for more than 50 years. The Chieftains -- founder Paddy Moloney, fiddle player and renowned solo artist Seán Keane, lead vocalist Kevin Conneff and flutist Matt Molloy -- are known for breaking many musical boundaries, sharing traditional Irish compositions in collaborations with some of the biggest names in popular music, such as The Rolling Stones and Madonna.

"Annie"
When: 7:30 p.m. Tuesday, April 5, and Wednesday, April 6
Cost: General public: $39 to $65; IU Bloomington students and children: $21 to $44
Synopsis: Leapin' Lizards! "Annie" returns to IU Auditorium with all the laughter, all the joy and all the heartfelt sentimentality that have inspired generations of readers, movie fans and theater-goers to fall in love with this timeless, rags-to-riches tale. Directed by original lyricist and director Martin Charnin and choreographed by IU alum Liza Gennaro, this production will be a brand-new incarnation of the original. Featuring book and score by Tony Award winners Thomas Meehan, Charles Strouse and Martin Charnin, "Annie" includes such unforgettable songs as "It's the Hard Knock Life" and "Tomorrow." Family friendly.

"Once"
When: 8 p.m. Tuesday, April 19 and Wednesday, April 20
Cost: General public: $39 to $65; IU Bloomington students: $21 to $44
Synopsis: This unique and inspiring musical theater event, based on the 2007 Academy Award-winning film, is propelled by its stunning ensemble of actor/musicians who perform their own instruments live on stage. Lauded for redefining the Broadway musical, "Once" tells the story of a street musician about to give up on his dreams when a beautiful young woman takes a sudden interest in his haunting love songs. As the chemistry between them grows, his music soars to powerful new heights, but their unlikely connection turns out to be deeper and more complex than your everyday romance.

"Bullets Over Broadway"
When: 8 p.m. Monday, May 2, and Tuesday, May 3,
Cost: General public: $39 to $65; IU Bloomington students: $21 to $44
Synopsis: Based on the screenplay of the acclaimed 1994 film by Woody Allen and Douglas McGrath, "Bullets Over Broadway" brings the talents of Allen and Tony-winning director Susan Stroman together for the first time. Hailed by Time magazine as "musical theatre gold," it tells the story of a young playwright who, in desperate need of financial backing for his next show, accepts an offer he can’t refuse from a mobster looking to please his showgirl girlfriend. Loaded with big laughs, colorful characters and the songs that made the 1920s roar, "Bullets Over Broadway" is bringing musical comedy back with a bang.

The IU Auditorium 2015-16 season is presented by Curry Auto Center, B97, the Herald-Times and The Cook Family of Companies (Cook Medical, CFC Properties and French Lick Resort).

To see the complete list of shows for the 2015-16 season, visit IUauditorium.com. The website features video clips and a selection of songs from next season’s performances, in addition to a direct link to the IU Auditorium Facebook page, Twitter page and email club, whose members receive exclusive benefits throughout the year. Tickets are available on the IU Auditorium website or by visiting the IU Auditorium box office, open 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Monday through Friday.
